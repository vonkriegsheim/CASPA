Species: Human
Tissue: Glioblastoma (GBM) tumour-associated neutrophils
Developmental stage: Adult
Condition: Surgical resection of GBM, 6 patients

Sample preparation: Tumour tissue enzymatically digested, then FACS sorted as
  CD45+CD66b+CD49d- to isolate neutrophils specifically. All cells in the dataset
  passed this neutrophil gate.

  All cells in the dataset are FACS-confirmed neutrophils (CD45+CD66b+CD49d-).

Instrument: Mass spectrometry-based single-cell proteomics, label-free DIA
Patients: 6 GBM patients, each patient = one batch

Known biology: Tumour-associated neutrophils (TANs) in GBM display diverse
  functional states. Neutrophil biology is profoundly post-transcriptional:
  granule proteins (MPO, ELANE, LTF, PRTN3, S100A8/A9) are synthesised during
  granulopoiesis and stored long after mRNAs are degraded.

Ambient signal warning: Neutrophil sample preparation releases granule contents,
  creating a pervasive ambient signal of granule proteins (MPO, ELANE, S100A8,
  S100A9, LTF, PRTN3) detected at ~1.00 in ALL clusters regardless of functional
  state. Detection rate for these proteins is non-discriminative; only INTENSITY
  fold-change distinguishes activated from depleted neutrophils.
