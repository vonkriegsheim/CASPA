Species: Mouse (Mus musculus)
Tissue: Pancreas
Developmental stage: Adult (8-12 weeks)
Condition: Caerulein-induced acute pancreatitis, harvested day 2 post-injury
Sample preparation: Enzymatic digestion, single-cell isolation, label-free DIA-MS
  on Orbitrap platform, Spectronaut search
Instrument: timsTOF SCP, Spectronaut search
Expected cell types: Acinar cells (injured, possibly undergoing acinar-to-ductal
  metaplasia/ADM), ductal epithelial cells (reactive/inflammatory), macrophages
  (tissue-resident Mrc1+ and infiltrating inflammatory), stellate cells (quiescent
  to activated myofibroblast gradient expected post-caerulein), inflammatory myeloid
  cells (neutrophils are early responders in caerulein pancreatitis), possibly
  lymphocytes (T cells, B cells at variable abundance), fibroblasts
Known biology: Caerulein pancreatitis triggers acute acinar cell injury with ER stress,
  ADM (acinar-to-ductal metaplasia), massive release of digestive enzymes into the
  interstitium, recruitment of neutrophils and monocytes, activation of pancreatic
  stellate cells along a quiescent→myofibroblast axis, and tissue remodelling.
  Day 2 is early injury phase — expect active inflammation, stellate activation,
  and acinar injury/regeneration programmes.
Ambient signal warning: Pancreatic acinar cells are packed with abundant digestive
  enzymes (Amy2a, Cpa1, Cpb1, Ctrb1, Cel, Pnlip, Prss1/2/3) that will be released
  during tissue digestion and detected as ambient carryover in ALL clusters regardless
  of cell type. These enzymes should NOT be used as evidence of acinar identity unless
  they show INTENSITY enrichment (not just detection) in a specific cluster.
